# SOCMINT Investigation & Sanitized Case Study

**Author:** uriel

## Overview
This repository contains a professional Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) and Social Media Intelligence (SOCMINT) reporting template, alongside a fully sanitized real-world case study. 

The purpose of this project is to demonstrate the intelligence-gathering lifecycle, from raw data collection to actionable, structured analysis, while strictly adhering to data privacy and operational security (OPSEC) best practices.

## Scenario & Objective
The included case study (`Sanitized_Report.md`) details a public exposure assessment of a target individual. 
* **Primary Objective:** To map the digital footprint, identify cross-platform username correlations, and assess potential OPSEC vulnerabilities based strictly on publicly available information.
* **Scope:** Passive reconnaissance only. No active engagement, bypassing of privacy controls, or credential harvesting was utilized.

## Methodology & Tools
The investigation followed a structured intelligence lifecycle (Direction, Collection, Processing, Analysis, and Dissemination). 

**Techniques Utilized:**
* **Cross-Platform Correlation:** Mapping root usernames and aliases across disparate platforms to build a unified identity profile.
* **Pattern-of-Life Analysis:** Analyzing timestamps and public location data to establish physical transit routines.
* **Secondary Network Analysis:** Leveraging public interactions from the target's associates to uncover historical data and geographic connections.
* **Advanced Search Operators:** Utilizing Google Dorks for targeted indexing.

**Tools Referenced:**
* OSINT Framework
* WhatsMyName
* Fazeel Azeez Channel Finder
* Lullar

## Key Analyst Takeaways
* **Data Sanitization:** The raw data was heavily scrubbed of Personally Identifiable Information (PII). Exact locations, dates of birth, real names, and specific URLs were abstracted to protect the target's identity while preserving the analytical value of the intelligence.
* **Risk Translation:** Raw findings were synthesized into tangible security risks, such as translating predictable departure routines into potential physical security considerations.

**This is a sanitized, educational case study.** Ethical conduct is the foundation of professional cybersecurity and intelligence work. The data in this report has been anonymized. I strictly adhere to professional rules of engagement: all OSINT activities documented here were conducted passively on publicly accessible data, respecting privacy boundaries and legal frameworks.
